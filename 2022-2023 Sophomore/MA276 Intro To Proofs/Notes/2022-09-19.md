## What about AND statements?

|<u>Givens</u>|<u>Goals</u>|
| :---: | :---: |
|$P$|$Q \land R$|

$$P \to (P\land R)$$
$$\equiv \neg P \lor (P \land R)$$
$$\equiv (\neg P \lor Q) \land (\neg P \lor R)$$
$$\equiv(P\to Q) \land (P \to R)$$

Now we split the goals up into:

|<u>Givens</u>|<u>Goals</u>|
| :---: | :---: |
|$P$|$Q$|
||$R$|

### Example
Prove that for every $n \in\mathbb{N}$ if $21 \mid n \to (7\mid n \land 3\mid n)$

<u>Proof:</u> Let $n \in \mathbb{N}$ such that $21 \mid n$. Then $\exists k \in \mathbb{Z}$ such that $n=21k$. Let $l=3k$ and $j=7k$. Then $n = 21k = 7(3k) = 7l$. Since $l \in \mathbb{Z}$, this tells us that $7\mid n$. Then $n=21k = 3(7k) = 3j$. Since $j\in\mathbb{Z}$, we know that $3\mid n.
\qquad\qquad\blacksquare$
